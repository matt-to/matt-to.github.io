About Matt

Matthew Tobin is a junior at Tappan Zee High School who hopes to use his skills and knowledge to help other people. He currently works as a tutor, with past employment in landscaping. He is also a certified lifeguard and a bagpiper with the Pearl River Ancient Order of Hibernians. In his free time, he enjoys reading about history, writing poetry, spending time with his family, and playing with his dogs. Matthew's main passion lies in public service. He ultimately hopes to study public policy and law, and then become a lawyer for the ACLU or the Justice Department. He always tries to be a worthwhile member of any group of which he is a part.

The entirety of this website was coded by Matthew. Digital proof for listed awards and positions has been provided where possible. References and additional information are available upon request.

Leadership

Youth and Government, 2017 - Present; Secretary of State, Nov. 2021 - Present

Matthew was first elected to leadership in 2017 as Youth Lieutenant Governor of the 250+ person conference. He has led his school's club since freshman year as Vice President. In 2021, he was appointed to the governor's cabinet as Commissioner of Transportation of the 600+ delegate conference. He has organized and conducted 100+ person conferences and coordinated meetings with State Senator Elijah Reichlin-Melnick, Assemblyman Mike Lawler, and NYS Commissioner of Transportation Theresa Dominguez. He is currently serving as Secretary of State, where he has led the cabinet, set up the pen pal program, and helped to plan and run the state conference. He has been chosen three times to be one of twenty-five NYS delegates to the ~500 person Conference on National Affairs (CONA), in 2020, 2021, and 2022, where his proposals for a national policy change were within the 10% that passed, two times so far.

Safe Routes, 2019 - Present; Club President, Dec. 2020 - Present

Matthew's interest in transportation began when he joined the YMCA-NYS Safe Routes Ambassador Program in May 2019. He also serves as the President of the Safe Routes Club, focused on improving local pedestrian transportation. He has led multiple community events to evaluate local walking conditions and met with officials to recommend improvements, such as the Orangetown Supervisor, the Highway Superintendent, and the Chief of Police. In June 2020, he was one of four student representatives on the South Orangetown Central School District's Reopening Task Force to plan the return to school post lockdown, with his area of responsibility being focused on student transportation.

Key Accomplishments:

  • Speaker at the Lifesavers National Conference on Highway Safety Priorities, April 2021
  • Published by the Safe Routes Partnership, October 2020
  • Successfully advocated for better pedestrian facilities at South Orangetown schools

Rockland County Youth Bureau Board of Directors, April 2022 - Present

The Rockland County Youth Bureau is responsible for overseeing and allocating fundings to youth programs in Rockland, as well as making nominations for various positions. After being recommended by a teacher, Matthew applied for the position and went through the process of being interviewed, being nominated by Rockland County Executive Ed Day, appearing in front of the Multi Services Committee, and being confirmed by the Rockland County Legislature. Matthew was sworn in on April 13th, 2022, and is one of two youth directors to serve on the board.

Drama Club, 2019 - Present; President, Sept. 2021 - Present

The Drama Club puts on two comedy skit shows every year in a production similar to Saturday Night Live. Matthew has been the author of numerous skits and an actor in many more. As club president, he is responsible for planning the show, making sure that all skits and actors were prepared, and promoting the show.

Family Engagment Center Ambassador, 2017 - Present

The Family Engagement Center was started in 2018 to promotes active parent involvement to support student success. Since its founding, Matthew has served as an ambassador. In this position, he runs community events and maintain community programs. For example, he is a volunteer at the district's food shelter, he helps new students transition to high school, he helped to start a daycare for the children of teachers during the lockdown, and every year, he help run the school's trunk-or-treat.

As a member of the Orangetown Youth Court, Matthew has been trained on the judicial system and puts what he has learned into action. He is responsible for hearing the cases of juvenile offenders and deciding on a sentence of community service. Matthew is a member of the Steering Committee and has served as a judge of the court.

Senator Elijah Reichlin-Melnick's Young Leaders Council, April 2021 - Present

Matthew was chosen to serve on the State Senator's Young Leaders Council in 2021. He meets on a regular basis with the senator to discuss issues that are imporant to his community and the youth. He also gets the opportunity to meet with other community leaders and elected officials to learn what work is being done for the community.

Boy Scouts

Matthew joined the Boy Scouts in 2016. During his time, he was a Patrol Leader for two years, Troop Scribe, and Senior Patrol Leader. He is currently a Junior Assistant Scoutmaster and a Life scout working on his Eagle Scout project, installing navigational signs on the Rail Trail.

Fencing

Matthew started fencing in eighth grade. He played primarily on Junior Varsity and as a substitute for the Varsity team. At the beginning of his sophomore year, he became a Varsity starter and has played there since. His team won 2nd place in the sectional competition in both 2020 and 2021 and 3rd place in 2022. Matthew was recognized with the Coach's Award in 2020.
