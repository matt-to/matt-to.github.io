About Matt

Matthew Tobin is a junior at Tappan Zee High School who hopes to use his skills and knowledge to help other people. He currently works as a tutor, with past employment in landscaping. He is also a certified lifeguard and a bagpiper with the Pearl River Ancient Order of Hibernians. In his free time, he enjoys reading about history, writing poetry, spending time with his family, and playing with his dogs. Matthew's main passion lies in public service. He ultimately hopes to study law and public policy, and to become a lawyer for the ACLU or the Justice Department. He always tries to be a worthwhile member of any group of which that he is a part.

The entirety of this website was coded by Matthew. Digital proof for listed awards and positions has been attached where possible. All references and information are available upon request.

Leadership

Youth and Government, 2017 - Present; Secretary of State, Nov. 2021 - Present

Elected to leadership starting in 7th grade as State Lieutenant Governor of the 250+ person conference. Led club since freshman year as Vice President. Appointed to governor’s cabinet as Commissioner of Transportation of the 600+ delegate conference in 2021. Organized and conducted 100+ person conferences. Safe Routes, 2019 - Present; Club President, Dec. 2020 - Present

My interest in transportation began when I joined the YMCA-NYS Safe Routes Ambassador Program in May 2019. I am also the President of the Safe Routes Club, focused on improving local pedestrian transportation. I have led multiple community events to evaluate local walking conditions and met with officials to recommend improvements, such as the Orangetown Supervisor, the Highway Superintendent, and the Chief of Police. In June 2020, I was one of four student representatives on my school district’s Reopening Task Force to plan the return to school post lockdown, with my area of responsibility being focused on student transportation.

Key Accomplishments:

  • Speaker at the Lifesavers National Conference on Highway Safety Priorities, April 2021
  • Published by the Safe Routes Partnership, October 2021

Drama Club, 2019 - Present; President, Sept. 2021 - Present

The Drama Club puts on two comedy skit shows every year in a production similar to Saturday Night Live. Matthew has been the author of numerous skits and an actor in many more. As club president, he is responsible for planning the show, making sure that all skits and actors were prepared, and promoting the show.

Family Engagment Center Ambassador, 2017 - Present

The Family Enrichment Center was started in 2018 to promotes active parent involvement to support student success. Since its founding, I have served as an ambassador. In this position, I run community events and maintain community programs. For example, I volunteer at the district’s food shelter, I help new students transition to high school, and I helped to start a daycare for the children of teachers during the lockdown, and every year, I help run the school's trunk-or-treat.

Matthew started fencing in 2018, his eighth grade year. He played primarily on Junior Varsity and as a substitute for the Varsity team. At the beginning of his sophomore year, he became a Varsity starter and has played there since. His team won 2nd place in both 2020 and 2021. Matthew was also recognized with the Coach's Award in 2020.
